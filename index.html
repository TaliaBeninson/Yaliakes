🗺️ Date 2: Dead Sea Detours

Some couples go on chill second dates. We accidentally auditioned for The Amazing Race: Israel Edition. First stop: מצפה ים המלח — which sounded like a great idea until the GPS took us on a scenic tour through half the Arab villages in the region. Add in a few of those bright red “NO ENTRY for Israelis” signs and suddenly we were starring in a suspense movie with zero backup plan.

Eventually, we reached the overlook — which turned out to be a sleepy little yeshuv with no sea in sight, minimal vibes, and just the right amount of “should we even be here?” existential crisis. Then we put our hopes in מצדה, dreaming of historical grandeur… only to find it locked up for the day.

But you? Totally unfazed. While I was internally Googling “how to survive in the Judean desert,” you just kept driving, calm and steady like this was all part of your master plan. (Was it?) We finally made it to the actual Dead Sea, took a walk, and casually stumbled into someone’s proposal. (No, I didn’t say yes. Tempting, though.)

Later, I taught you Wordle (you're welcome). You tried to take me to your favorite spot in מחנה יהודה, but parking said “Not today,” so we rerouted to סינמה סיטי and ended up at החברים. Nothing went as planned, but somehow everything felt exactly right.

Date Rating: Drama: 10/10. Navigation: -infinity. You: suspiciously chill.

BUT — and this is important — I did download a puzzle game on the car screen because I love puzzles, and honestly? That counts as beach-adjacent bonding in my book. Just two people, one car, zero sand, and a whole lot of conversation.

Date Rating: Escape skills: elite. Beach walk execution: fail. Car park intimacy: 10/10.
